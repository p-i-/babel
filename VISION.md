## The north star

*The Young Lady's Illustrated Primer* (Neal Stephenson, *The Diamond Age*): a book
commissioned to teach one child everything she needs to rise and thrive — adaptive,
patient, illustrated from the whole world's material, bound to its student by genuine
rapport. Stephenson needed a hidden human behind its voice. We don't. The voice, the
eyes, the improvisation are now rentable; what remained to build was the *teacher* —
the memory, the method, the workshop — and that is babel.

Language tutoring is the beachhead, not the boundary. A teacher that knows you, keeps
its own notes, and can summon any material from the world can teach geography in your
native tongue as naturally as vocabulary in a new one. The Primer took responsibility
for a rounded education. So, eventually, can this.

## What we believe about learning

- **Recall is the gameplay.** Every effective mechanic — flashcards, memory palaces,
  text adventures, roleplay with a stubborn waiter — is a disguise for effortful
  retrieval at spaced intervals. The disguise matters (motivation, delight); the
  active ingredient never changes. Test of any feature idea: *does it cause recall,
  or just pleasant exposure?*
- **Learn like a child with a toy.** A child experiences a new thing every possible
  way — looks, grabs, tastes, throws. Every language item deserves the same: heard,
  spoken, read, typed, written by hand, used in play. Multimodality is not garnish;
  in babel it is a *measured, scheduled property* of every item.
- **The distractor is the mechanism.** Material must stop buzzing in the head before
  revisiting it does any good. Go away, come back: each item touched on consecutive
  days — learn, reinforce, polish — riding sleep twice (the rolling window: A, AB,
  ABC, BCD…).
- **Unlimited talking time at zero affective filter.** A classroom gives a student
  two minutes of speech an hour and an audience for their mistakes. A private tutor
  gives thirty-plus, with infinite patience and no embarrassment. This alone — before
  any clever feature — is the structural win.
- **Honesty over flattery.** Empty praise teaches nothing and erodes trust. Name
  what was wrong precisely, require another attempt, praise sparingly so it means
  something. Rapport comes from being warm AND rigorous; students tune out flatterers.
- **Silence is not awkward.** A teacher who fills every pause is serving their own
  comfort. If there is nothing worth saying, say nothing; results and thoughts arrive
  better into quiet.

## What we believe about building

- **A clean core; push complexity outward — or evaporate it.** The entire system is:
  *a voice, a stage, an eye, a memory, Python, and one verb.* Anything that can move
  from code into tooling-plus-prompt, should. Four bespoke tools became one; the
  whiteboard became a template the teacher owns; memory became notes the teacher
  designs.
- **A teaching platform with no content.** No lesson files, no curriculum database,
  no recorded audio. Primitives + principles + a student model; every lesson is
  generated fresh, personalized, in any of seventy languages. The marginal cost of a
  new language is a voice check and a keyboard layout.
- **The teacher owns its tools and its memory.** We seed `helpers.py` and hand over
  the pen. Its board template, its note schema, its little scripts — teacher-authored,
  teacher-evolved. We build the workshop, not the work.
- **The board is working memory; the notes are long-term memory.** Never teach an
  invisible word; never end a lesson unwritten.
- **Information flow is the product.** The agent must see what the student
  experiences (events, drawings, keystrokes, cutoffs) and must surface what it
  experiences (glitches, surprises) — transparency in both directions, the same
  virtue as good logging. And prune the flow: never tell it the same bad news twice.
- **Verify empirically; trust no recollection.** Advisors, dashboards, and vendor
  docs have each been wrong at least once in this project's short life. The probe is
  the authority. Build disposable toys, run them, read the diagnostics, fold the
  learnings back in.

## What "better than any classroom" means

A teacher with perfect memory of every stumble you ever made; who schedules each
word's return the day your brain is about to lose it; who writes you Seussian verse
for the exact phoneme you can't hear; who summons a real Kyiv café menu when you're
ready to read one; who plays the stubborn waiter until you repair your own sentence;
who notices you light up at astronomy and teaches you the genitive case with black
holes — and who, when you say goodnight, writes up its notes like any teacher who
loves the work, and remembers you in the morning.
